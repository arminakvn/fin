In the city of Boston, the nightlife is not as prominent as it is in other cities but in contrast to other cities Boston has the potential and the student base to benefit from a more lively nightlife. This project is intended to present various transit options that run later in the evening in order to promote a more active nightlife keeping in mind the safety and interest of the people who reside in the Boston area. How we plan on measuring this information (the interest of Boston locals) is with the data from the MBTA website which gives you in detail who uses the lines, when they are used more frequently, and which lines and stations have more traffic. The information drawn from this will allow us to have a better idea of to who and what the city could potentially cater to.
The city could potentially reach a level of profit rivaling that of other large cities that have a thriving night scene along with certain measures implemented to promote these conducts, such as transportation or business premises that are open at night and do most of their business after nightfall, particularly in the early hours of the morning (e.g. 1-3 AM) while some areas in the city may be strictly residential.

For this project, our group decided to focus on data retrieved from the MBTA website detailing the frequency of usage for the different lines.
